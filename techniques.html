Getting Started

Relax... Meditation comes in many forms and reeps benefits to the practitioner. To start, find a quiet place with little distraction for you to dedicate your time for your meditation.

It is normal for the mind to wander at the beginning of your journey. Be patient with yourself and think of it as a marathon and not a sprint. You wouldn't run a marathon without training first so think of your meditation as training for the longer practices in the future.

See our postures page for recommendations on how to make yourself comfortable for your practice.

Below are some options to help you choose which one is best for you and your practice. You can also vary your pratice by changing your technique.

Counting

What is it?

It is exactly what it sounds like. During this practice you will be focussing your attention on something other than your thoughts. Your mind will naturally wander so this method is a good practice to help bring the attention back to focussing on something other than distracting thoughts.

Benefits
  • Good beginners technique
  • Strengthens the focus of the mind
  • Easy to develop into longer meditations
Methods
Breath Counting

In your comfortable meditation posture with eyes closed and after your conditioning. Start to count the breaths on the inhale and the exhale up to number of 10 and repeat. eg. Inhale is 1, exhale is 2, inhale is 3 etc. Repeat this method for 5 minutes.

Numerical Counting

In your comfortable meditation posture with eyes closed and after your conditioning. Start to count freely in your head from 1 up to 100. Repeat this method for 5 minutes. You can also practice this in reverse.

Focus

What is it?

The focus meditation also know as Samatha or focussed attention meditation (FAM) in some practices is a practice where the practitioner focusses the attention on a thought, sound, sensation or object of their choosing. An early example of this is the fire gazing meditation mentioned previously on this website. Another good practice where if the mind wanders the practitioner can bring the attention back to their chosen focus for this meditation.

Benefits
  • Good beginners technique
  • Helps you stay present
  • Increases focus and concentration
Methods
Candle

In your comfortable meditation posture with eyes closed and after your conditioning. Have a lit candle placed in front of you. Without damaging your sight gently focus your gaze on the flame for the duration of your practice. If the mind wanders, bring your focus back to the candle.

Sensation

In your comfortable meditation posture with eyes closed and after your conditioning. Bring your attention to the sensation of the contact of your body with the floor and with the feeling of your breath moving in your body.

Noting

What is it?

Noting or mental noting is a technique used to help the practitioner notice and let go of distractions by practicing awareness of thoughts, sensations and sounds that have caused the practitioner to lose focus. The meditator can then give these a label to help them recognise and develop a method of detachment from distracting thoughts, sensations and sounds.

Benefits
  • Helps create awareness and strengthen focus
  • Develops a deeper awareness of the mind
  • Healthy detachment from distractions
Method

In your comfortable meditation posture with eyes closed and after your conditioning. Bring your attention to your breath as your focus for your meditation. When you hear a sound make a mental note with the word "sound". When you have a thought make a mental note with the word "thought". When you notice a sensation in your body make a mental note with the word "sensation". Try not to let the things you notice become a distraction as you will lose focus but try to bring yourself back to focussing on your breath after you have made a mental note.
